The setup

This is the exciting story of how we built a pinhole camera, took touristy pictures, and learned about photography.

We started off in the Cory makerspace. That's where we found all of the things we'd need to build a miniature dark room:

It took a fair amount of measuring and marking, as well as some poking and padding, but we successfully assembled our box. Here it is:

At this point we should note that James Chang graciously donated his Canon EOS-M to us, and that we couldn't have done this project without it. We feel a little guilty about putting it in so many precarious positions. Sorry James!

One further note: we used ImageMagick to transpose our images but we haven't done any other post-processing on the images you're about to see.

Light painting

We needed to find a really dark room to try light painting. My closet was perfect for the job. Here's what it looked like when lit (left), and with the lights turned out (right).

It was pretty spooky in there but I didn't let it get to me. Fearlessly, I started painting with light:

Here we see a loop, the symbol for existential quantifiers, a collection of sine waves, and the letter V (in clockwise order). Unfortunately, I couldn't manage anything much more complicated because it's hard to draw transposed pictures in a cramped space. I'm glad that we got really crisp light paintings though.

Note: The top-right image is brighter than the other three because it was intentionally overexposed (with a higher ISO sensitivity and wider aperture). This causes an interesting blurring effect, effectively widening my light paintbrush (read: iPhone flashlight).
